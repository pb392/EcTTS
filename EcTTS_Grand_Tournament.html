Grand Tournament is a satirical game with the original idea to be built around the ideology of "If everything is broken, nothing is broken." Grand Tournament is built on adapting stories and pop culture into a fantastical version of the original, reimagining them in various different ways. The game itself is structured as a 5 on 5 board, where players have their Hero Card in its own zone, and 5 playable zones in front of the Hero. Players are essentially telling a story while playing the game.

The game has a few different things to be aware of when building a deck. The Hero Deck is a big necessity with requiring a total of 6 Heroes, up to 1 Basic Hero to start with and a collection of up to 5 other Hero cards, Epic or Basic, within the Hero Deck. Having 1 Villain card can replace the need for an entire Hero deck. The Basic Hero, or Villain, determines the Elements allowed into the Main Deck, but not the Hero Deck. With 6 Elements, and a Void Element allowing for all Elements to be combined at once, there's endless possibilities.

The Main Deck has a minimum of 40 cards and a maximum of 50 cards, and can have up to 3 of a singular card. As well as being able to play any Element card that coincides with your Original Basic Hero's Elements, the Story you tell will truly be your own. Travel the Coliseum's Archive into the countless stories to amass a fighting force to call your own and fight to be called Number 1.
